What is this study about?

As a participant of this study, you will teach a computer agent how to solve a game. In order to teach the agent how to win a game, you will provide the agent with negative or positive feedback. This feedback will help the agent understand when it's doing a good or bad job.

To help you, the participant, provide feedback most effectively to the agent, we are suggesting you follow the strategy described below:

We suggest you use the uniform advising strategy. This strategy involves you continually providing feedback to the agent at a steady rate as it interacts in the game.
In the strategy, you do not want to restrict your feedback to specific locations of the game environment or at specific points in time.
