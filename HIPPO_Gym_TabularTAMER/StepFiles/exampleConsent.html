What is this study about?

As a participant of this study, you will teach a computer agent how to solve 2 games. In order to teach the agent how to win a game, you will provide the agent with negative or positive feedback. This feedback will help the agent understand when it's doing a good or bad job.

To help you, the participant, provide feedback most effectively to the agent, we are suggesting you follow the strategy described below:

We suggest you use the importance advising strategy. This strategy involves you providing feedback to the agent only at important places in the game.
By important, we mean areas in the game where:

1. The wrong action can lead the agent to have a major setback or possibly lose the game.

2. The right action can lead the agent to have a greater chance of winning the game.
